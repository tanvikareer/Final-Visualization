This is the home of the Banjara community


The Banjaras are an agricultural community. Traditionally a nomadic community, they are now small holder farmers or landless laborers.


In this community, the landless women are the most vulnerable, as they work on a daily wage of less than $2 per day. Not only is their income low, but it is also unpredictable, as the amount of work they get changes from week to week.


This visualisation shows the various ways in which the land owning and landless families spend money.



One of the big expenses of the land owning families is paying off loans, which is completely absent in the land less families.

Out of the 461 families in the village, only 6.5% are a part of a Self Helf Savings Group.


This clearly shows that the landless families are not availing of any of the formal saving methods available in the village. Thus, these women who have very limited and unpredictable incomes, also do not have adequate tools to save and invest their income. My thesis will explore what could be some new ways to make this process accessible, tangible, flexible, personalised and fun for these women.
